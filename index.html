About

Pauline is an urban planner and architect with over eight years of professional experience in participatory design, data analysis, local climate adaptation, and community engagement. She has worked with the private sector, international development agencies, local governments, and non-profit organizations focusing on vulnerable communities. Pauline has developed projects in Chile, United States, Angola, Mozambique, Dominican Republic, and Puerto Rico. She holds an MS. degree in Urban Planning from Columbia University and an Architect professional degree from Universidad de Chile. She is currently working at AECOM in New York City.

Gowanus Commons

Building Resilient Communities.

Gowanus Commons presents a strategy to increase disaster response and preparedness at a neighborhood scale developed with the Urban Planning Studio 2018 at Columbia University in partnership with the New York City Emergency Management Office (NYCEM).The project tailored the existing community emergency planning toolkit developed by NYCEM for the Gowanus Community in Brooklyn, incorporating site-specific assessment for various hazards, stakeholder analysis to identify key actors, and community engagement tools. We designed and tested engagement tools with local actors producing a planning toolkit and a template for an App to enhance community preparedness.

  • Date: Spring 2018
  • Client: New York City Emergency Management Office
  • See the full report to explore maps and outreach strategies at https://issuu.com/pclaramunt/docs/gowanus_commons
